Skill Acquisition/Replacement Goals

These goals will be accomplished using shaping and chaining procedures accompanied by most-to least promptings and level of assistance. Level of assistance will depend on the client’s needs and the nature of the behavioral goal. Level of assistance will be indicated in the instruction section of each program data collection form. Programs for Alternative Behaviors should also be implemented using Incidental Teaching, meaning at every opportunity of teaching these behaviors is presented while services are provided.
